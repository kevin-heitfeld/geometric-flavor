## 1. Standard Model Parameters (~26 unexplained numbers)

### Fermion Masses (9 parameters)
- **Electron mass**: me = 0.511 MeV - why this value?
- **Muon mass**: mμ = 105.7 MeV - why 207× electron?
- **Tau mass**: mτ = 1777 MeV - why 3477× electron?
- **Up quark mass**: mu ≈ 2.2 MeV
- **Down quark mass**: md ≈ 4.7 MeV
- **Strange quark mass**: ms ≈ 95 MeV
- **Charm quark mass**: mc ≈ 1.3 GeV
- **Bottom quark mass**: mb ≈ 4.2 GeV
- **Top quark mass**: mt ≈ 173 GeV - why so heavy?

### Quark Mixing - CKM Matrix (4 parameters)
- **θ12** (Cabibbo angle) ≈ 13°
- **θ13** ≈ 0.2°
- **θ23** ≈ 2.4°
- **δCP** (CP-violating phase) ≈ 69°
- Why this particular pattern of mixing?

### Neutrino Sector (7 parameters)
- **Three neutrino masses**: m1, m2, m3 (only differences measured)
- **PMNS mixing angles**: θ12 ≈ 34°, θ23 ≈ 42°, θ13 ≈ 8.5°
- **CP-violating phase**: δCP ≈ 230° (poorly constrained)
- **Mass ordering**: Normal or inverted hierarchy?
- **Dirac or Majorana**: Are neutrinos their own antiparticles?
- **Why so light**: Why ~0.1 eV vs. GeV for other fermions?

### Gauge Couplings (3 parameters)
- **Strong coupling**: αs(MZ) ≈ 0.118
- **Weak coupling**: Related to sin²θW ≈ 0.231
- **Electromagnetic coupling**: α ≈ 1/137.036
- Why these specific values?
- Do they unify at high energy?

### Higgs Sector (2 parameters)
- **Higgs mass**: mH = 125 GeV - why this value?
- **Vacuum expectation value**: v = 246 GeV
- Why is Higgs so light? (Hierarchy problem)

### QCD Parameters
- **QCD theta angle**: θQCD < 10⁻¹⁰ - why so small? (Strong CP problem)
- **QCD scale**: ΛQCD ≈ 200 MeV - why this value?

- Small dimensionless numbers like 10⁻¹²²
- Large hierarchies like 10¹⁷

**What's Missing**:
- Either naturalness is wrong as a principle
- Or we're missing stabilization mechanisms
- Or anthropic selection is the answer (disturbing but possible)

**The Naturalness Crisis**: LHC found Higgs at 125 GeV with no supersymmetry to stabilize it. This suggests either fine-tuning is acceptable, or we're missing something.
